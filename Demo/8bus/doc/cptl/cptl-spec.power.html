Abstract

This specification describes an extension to the CPTL Vocabulary for the bulk electric system. The extension is defined as a dictionary of named properties and classes using W3C's RDF technology. We note that the format and scope of this specification is based directly off of the FOAF Vocabulary Specification.

The Cyber Physical Topology Language (CPTL) is a project whose main objective is to provide a human-readable, machine-actionable language for practitioners and researchers to communicate information about cyber-physical systems across different domains (e.g. electrical power, enterprise networks, smart cities). The etymology of the word system (σύστημα) means an organized whole; body and as such, graphs a natural way to formally represent different assets and relations among them within a cyber-physical system. More information about the CPTL Vocabulary in general may be found in the CPTL Vocabulary Spec.

The CPTL Power Vocabulary defines terms that may be employed to construct several different views or semantic graphs, on the electrical power grid.

  • substation network: Devices and connections among devices found in an electrical substation network. These include relays of different types.
  • substation yard: Components and connections among components found in a substation yard. These include but are not limited to breakers as well as transformers.

    Classes & Properties

    Substation Network

    Class: snet:DistanceRelay

    DistanceRelay - The most common form of protection on high voltage transmission systems is distance relay protection. Power lines have set impedance per kilometre and using this value and comparing voltage and current the distance to a fault can be determined. (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protective_relay#Distance_relay)

    Class: snet:OvercurrentRelay

    OvercurrentRelay - A digital over current relay is a protective relay which operates when the load current exceeds a pickup value (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protective_relay#Over_current_relay)

    Class: snet:RTU

    RTU - A remote terminal unit (RTU) is a microprocessor-controlled electronic device that interfaces objects in the physical world to a SCADA ssytem by transmitting telemetry data to a master system. (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remote_Terminal_Unit)

    Class: snet:Relay

    Relay - A device designed to trip a circuit breaker when a fault is detected. (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protective_relay)

    Class: snet:ReversePowerRelay

    ReversePowerRelay - A reverse power relay is a directional power relay that is used to monitor the power from a generator running in parallel with another generator or the utility. The function of the reverse power relay is to prevent a reverse power condition in which power flows from the bus bar into the generator. This condition can occur when there is a failure in the prime mover such as an engine or a turbine which drives the generator. (http://www.electrotechnik.net/2009/06/reverse-power-relay-function-and.html)

    Class: snet:Switch

    Switch - A network switch (also called switching hub, bridging hub, officially MAC bridge[1]) is a computer networking device that connects devices together on a computer network, by using packet switching to receive, process and forward data to the destination device. Unlike less advanced network hubs, a network switch forwards data only to one or multiple devices that need to receive it, rather than broadcasting the same data out of each of its ports. (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Network_switch)

    Property: snet:hasBreakerConnections

    hasBreakerConnections - Connections from a relay to a breaker consist of (1) high-side voltage (2) low-side voltage, (3) a current loop, (4) breaker status, and (5) a breaker control line.

    Substation Yard

    Class: syard:Breaker

    Breaker - A breaker is an automatically operated electrical switch designed to protect an electrical circuit from damage caused by overload or short circuit (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circuit_breaker).

    Class: syard:Bus

    Bus - A busbar is a strip or bar of conductive material designed to conduct a substantial current of electricity (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Busbar).

    Class: syard:Generator

    Generator - A generator is a device that converts mechanical energy to electrical energy for use in an external circuit. Generators provide nearly all of the power for electric power grids (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_generator).

    Class: syard:Node

    Node - Not sure whether this really belongs here or in an ontology for node-breaker diagrams.

    Class: syard:SubstationYardComponent

    SubstationYardComponent - Any piece of switching, protection, or control equipment in a substation yard. This may also include other assets such as a fence or substation house. (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electrical_substation)

    Class: syard:Transformer

    Transformer - A transformer is an electrical device that transfers energy between two or more circuits through electromagnetic induction (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transformer).

    Property: syard:hasLine

    hasLine - An electrical power line between two substation yard components.
